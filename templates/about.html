What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere, which can affect human health, the environment, and the climate. Common sources include vehicle emissions, industrial discharge, construction, and burning of fossil fuels.

Key Air Pollutants

  • PM2.5: Fine inhalable particles with diameters ≤ 2.5 micrometers. Highly dangerous for lungs and heart.
  • PM10: Particles with diameters ≤ 10 micrometers. Causes respiratory issues.
  • NO₂: Emitted by vehicles and power plants; worsens asthma.
  • SO₂: Comes from burning coal and oil; affects lungs.
  • CO: A colorless gas from combustion, deadly in high amounts.
  • O₃ (Ozone): Ground-level ozone forms from other pollutants and causes chest pain, coughing, and lung problems.

How is Air Quality Measured?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) converts concentrations of pollutants into a scale from 0 to 500. The higher the AQI, the more hazardous the air. Our model predicts the AQI based on historical data in Phnom Penh.

How to Protect Yourself During Air Pollution Events

When air quality deteriorates, it's crucial to take proactive steps to minimize your exposure and safeguard your health. Here are some practical guidelines:

  • Stay Indoors: Remain inside, especially during peak pollution hours (typically early morning or evening). Use air purifiers if available.
  • Close Windows & Doors: Seal your living space to prevent outdoor pollutants from entering.
  • Use N95 or KN95 Masks: These masks can filter fine particles (PM2.5), unlike regular cloth or surgical masks.
  • Limit Physical Activity: Avoid strenuous activities like running or cycling outdoors when AQI is high.
  • Monitor Air Quality: Regularly check air quality reports using apps or systems like this detector to make informed decisions.
  • Stay Hydrated and Eat Antioxidants: Water flushes toxins, and foods rich in vitamins C and E can help reduce inflammation.
  • Consult a Doctor: Those with asthma, heart issues, or respiratory conditions should follow medical advice closely during smog events.

Global Actions to Reduce Air Pollution

Air pollution is a shared global issue requiring both personal responsibility and systemic change. Here are key strategies at various levels:

  • Transition to Renewable Energy: Replace coal and fossil fuels with solar, wind, and hydroelectric energy sources to reduce emissions.
  • Promote Sustainable Transport: Use public transport, bike, walk, or drive electric vehicles to cut vehicular emissions.
  • Strengthen Environmental Policy: Governments must enforce stricter air quality regulations and industrial emission standards.
  • Urban Green Spaces: Planting trees and maintaining parks helps absorb carbon dioxide and particulate matter.
  • Waste Management: Avoid open burning of waste and promote recycling to reduce harmful smoke and chemicals.
  • Public Awareness Campaigns: Educating communities about pollution sources and solutions is key to behavioral change.
  • Invest in Clean Technologies: Innovation in cleaner manufacturing and energy systems can make industries more eco-friendly.

Together, these steps form a roadmap toward cleaner air and a healthier planet. Your awareness—and action—matters.
